## Day 13 â€“ Channel-wise Sales & Profit Performance Analysis

### Objective
Analyze **Sales and Profit across different sales channels** to understand how revenue contribution aligns with profitability and identify channel-level efficiency and risk.

While MoM analysis explains *how performance changes over time*, channel analysis focuses on *where performance is generated within the business*.

---

### Business Questions Answered
- Which sales channels contribute the most to total revenue?
- Are high-revenue channels also the most profitable?
- Which channels show efficiency gaps or underperformance?
- Where does channel dependency or business risk exist?

---

### Work Performed
- Aggregated Total Sales by Channel  
- Aggregated Total Profit by Channel  
- Compared volume-driven vs efficiency-driven channels  
- Visualized channel-wise Sales and Profit using bar charts  
- Evaluated channel contribution to overall business performance  

---

### Key Insights
- Wholesale is the dominant channel, contributing the highest share of both Sales and Profit  
- Distributor shows balanced and stable performance  
- Export underperforms in both revenue and profitability  
- Revenue contribution and profitability are not evenly distributed across channels  
- Heavy reliance on a single channel increases business dependency risk  

---

### Business Impact
This analysis enables the business to:
- Prioritize high-performing sales channels  
- Identify channels causing potential profit leakage  
- Optimize pricing and discount strategies by channel  
- Reduce dependency risk through better channel mix planning  

---

### Recommended Business Solutions
- Strengthen high-performing channels to maximize revenue impact  
- Optimize Distributor channel to maintain stable margins  
- Re-evaluate Export channel pricing, logistics, and market demand  
- Diversify channel strategy to improve long-term business stability  

---

- Microsoft Excel
