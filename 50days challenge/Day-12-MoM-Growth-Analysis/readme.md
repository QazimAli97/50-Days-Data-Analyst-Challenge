## Objective
Analyze **Month-over-Month (MoM) growth** in Sales and Profit to understand performance **volatility, momentum, and business risk** across consecutive months.

While seasonality highlights *when* demand is high or low, MoM analysis focuses on *how quickly* performance changes.

---

## Business Questions Answered
- How stable is monthly business performance?
- Which months experience sharp drops or recoveries?
- Is profit growth more volatile than sales growth?
- Where do early warning signals appear?

---

## Work Performed

- Aggregated **monthly Sales and Profit**
- Calculated **MoM growth %** for Sales and Profit
- Identified high-volatility and stable periods
- Visualized MoM growth using column charts
- Compared Sales vs Profit volatility

---

## Key Insights

- Month-over-month growth is **not linear** and shows clear volatility
- Sharp declines appear after peak periods, signaling potential risk
- Profit growth is **more volatile than sales**, indicating margin sensitivity
- Stable months provide a reliable baseline for forecasting
- Large swings highlight periods requiring deeper investigation

---

## Business Impact

This analysis enables the business to:
- Detect early warning signals of demand slowdowns
- Improve forecasting accuracy by accounting for volatility
- Identify months with higher profitability risk
- Take proactive corrective actions instead of reactive decisions

---

## Recommended Business Solutions

- Implement **MoM monitoring** to flag sudden declines early
- Adjust forecasts dynamically for high-volatility months
- Review pricing, discounts, and cost structures during profit swings
- Align inventory, staffing, and operational planning with volatility patterns
